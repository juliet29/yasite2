VALUES

Our values, what we stand for, are quite literally
the foundation of the JC Ibekaku Hope Project. Our
objectives and core mandates are critical to our success.

Objectives guide us.

The J C Ibekaku Hope Project has discovered that unemployment among our graduate youths is causing night mare to all levels of government in this country, the parents and the youths in particular. It is even saddening that where opportunity exists, many factors come to play, these factors are highly discriminatory; age limit, years of experience, tribe and above all, “who do you know?”

Because of these discriminatory factors, the youths have begun to fear tomorrow and they believe that the promise of being leaders of tomorrow is a myth. This fear of tomorrow has created multiple indices in the minds of the youths; some have taken to such vices that not only destroy their targets, but also to themselves later on. It is on this premises that the J. C. Ibekaku Hope Project makes it a primary objective to give “Hope” to these “seemingly” hopeless ones.

Our core mandates are as follows:

  • Opportunity

  • Maintaining educational opportunities for the indigent through its scholarship and bursary schemes

  • Business

  • Catering to the needs of rural women through its micro-credit schemes

  • Independence

  • Promoting economic independence among women and youth through our skills acquisition center and other training programs

  • Healthcare

  • Providing free health care services in areas where they are scarce

  • Employment

  • Ehancing employment opportunities for graduates through the conduct of seminars, workshops and specialized training sessions

  • Collaboration

  • Working in partnership with organizations of similar objectives to achieve goals

  • Community

  • Highlighting the importance of participation by women and youth in national development and social change

  • Scholarship

  • Hepling students seeking university admission locally and internationally
