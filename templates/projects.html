Essay Competition.

Essay Competition. Strengthening the minds of tommorrow with essay writing today.

Summary

At inception of J.C. Ibekaku Hope project, it was discovered that student’s unionisim which is a veritable instrument of preparing our youths towards leadership position in the society has become moribund in Enugu state. The side effects were so enormous, that our student cannot compete favorably with other students in leadership positions in their various universities and other higher institutions of learning, where they existed, their impacts cannot be felt by the students themselves, the obvious reason being that no one has discovered this laxity and offered to assist, this basically is the connection of J. C. Ibekaku Hope Project to bringing to bear the institution of JCHP Annual Essay Competition Awards.

Results

The First JCHIP Annual Essay Competition Awards which topic entitled “Transforming local community to a city: policy issues to consider.” was written by a total number of One hundred and Twenty-Three participants. Award winners of the Essay Competition who are Eze Nneka 1st position from Igbo-Eze North LGA, 2nd Position Eze Obinna from Affa in UDI LGA and 3rd position Ani Solomon from Nkanu-West LGA were given the sum of fifteen thousand naira, ten thousand naira and seven thousand naira respectively.

The Second edition of the JCHIP Annual Essay Competition Awards which topic entitled “PARADIGM SHIFT IN LEADERSHIP IN NIGERIA; WHITHER NIGERIA YOUTHS was written by a total number of One Hundred and Eighty Two participants.

Dr. Cephas Lerewonu, the chairman of the ERC stated that many factors were considered during the selection of the Winners of the Essay competition. He equally thanked the panelists for their efforts during the screening of the Essays. The Essay Competition winners are; Francis Chieze from Egede in Udi LGA won the 1st Prize and given a cash prize of N10,000.00 and Samsung Galaxy 4. Ugwu Joel Chinonso from Ibagwa-Aku in Igboeze-South LGA won the 2nd Prize and given a cash prize of N10,000.00 and Blackberry note pad. Josephine Chinasa Awoke from Umulumgbe in Udi LGA won the 3rd prize and given a cash prize of N10,000.00 only.

Future Goals

The anticipation of the Essay Awards was that it would go a long way to improving the literary, writing and reading skills of the participants.

The Organization equally sponsored the publication of a book entitled Nigeria After Fifty written by Rev. Kelvin Eze. The book was launched in during the JCIHP 3rd Annual Leadership Seminar held on 2nd August, 2013. The Organization’s quest on this is to ensure the youths take over the stage of publication from Wole Soyinka, late Chinua Achebe and host of other writers.

Seminar Training. Preparing graduates to enter the workforce.

Summary

One of our most prevalent projects was a 4- day Workshop organized for unemployed graduates’ youths in Enugu, on the 5th - 8th of March 2012 at Fontana Hotels, Enugu. Over 70 unemployed graduates selected from the 17 Local Government Areas in Enugu and youths from Anambra, Ebonyi and Imo State participated in the programme. The essence of the training was to improve the graduates to be employable to the nation. At the end of the workshop, the participants were able to draft a standard curriculum vitae and preparatory requirement for attending interviews. The seminar was aired on Radio Nigeria/ESBS and equally published in the News Bulletin of Ministry of Information, Enugu.

Annual Leadership Day.

jcihp

 ×

Annual Leadership Day. Helping youth fulfill their potential.

Summary

The J.C. Ibekaku Hope Project has organized three leadership seminars which started from 2011 to date (2013).

The second in series was organized in collaboration with Egede Students Union at Ogba-Wushi Egbe Hall, Egede on 15 September, 2012. The Chairman of the occasion, Prince Augustine Eze stated that the youths were specially endowed with different potentials and expressed the need to harness these attributes. The key note remarks were delivered by Mr. Stanley Anyadi, Special Assistant to Deputy Senate President, Ike Ekweremadu on Information Technology on the theme, “Transforming Egede Youths through Information and Communication Technology”. A total number of 308 undergraduates and graduates from Affa, Egede, Umuoka, Umulumgbe in Udi and Ezeagu LGA attended the function.

The third in series was organized in collaboration with Youths Alliance for Peaceful Democratic Change on 2nd August, 2013 at Citi-Park Luxury Hotel, Independence Layout Enugu.

Results

The objectives of the one day leadership seminar are; to prepare the youths by building their leadership skill, to develop programs toward youths empowerment, to improve youths access to education and to reduce youths unemployment and restiveness in Enugu State. A total number of 261 undergraduates and graduates from the 17 Local Government Area of Enugu State attended the workshop. Also in attendance were the Honorable Commissioner for Gender Affairs, Enugu State representing the State Governor, the State Youth Leader pf the Peoples Democratic Party (PDP) Dr. Christian Iyiani, who also delivered paper on Youths Unemployment in Nigeria: The Bane of Rascality to Nation at the occasion.

Future Goals

At the end of the workshop, the participants through the president of the National Council of Enugu State Students (NCESS) presented to the founder of J C Ibekaku Hope project, Barr. Juliet Ibekaku an award as Patroness of the Association for fostering political peace and making valuable investment in the life of youths.

Summary

Health Care Services. Celebrating International Womens' Day with health care awareness.

Summary

This organization on March 8, 2013 being the International Women’s Day, embarked on a program tagged “Free Health Care for Rural Women” in collaboration with volunteer doctors and nurses lead by Dr. Ngozi Madukaife of the University Teaching Hospital, Abakiliki, Ebonyi State. Though meant for promoting good health for the women, no doubt, the men were not left out in the exercise. A total number of Two Hundred and Fifty Women and Forty Five Men were administered free drugs after general test exercises being conducted on them. The JCIHP free health care services have been extended to Nze and Ngwo in Udi Local Govt. Area.

Results

After the Annual Leadership in 2nd August, 2013, free HIV & Hepatitis B test were carried out on One Hundred and Fifteen participate that voluntarily submitted themselves for the test.

The J.C Ibekaku Hope project through its health care services catered for the medical expenses for brain surgery of the daughter of one Mrs. Eucharia Obianife, a resident in Oji-River LGA.
